Routine maintenance is the key to keeping your vehicle reliable, safe, and on the road for a long time. Follow this guide to understand and keep track of essential maintenance tasks.

## Essential Maintenance Tips

<CardGroup>
  <Card title="Check Your Oil" icon="oil-can">
    Keep your engine running smoothly by checking the oil level monthly and changing it as per your maintenance schedule.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tire Pressure and Treads" icon="tire">
    Check tire pressure monthly and inspect for tread wear. Rotate tires and have alignments checked every 6,000 miles.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Brake Inspection" icon="brake-pedal">
    Listen for any brake noise and get your brakes checked if you notice anything unusual.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Seasonal Checks

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Spring">
    Test your air conditioning and check for any servicing needs before the weather gets hot.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Summer">
    Inspect your cooling system to prevent overheating during hot months.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Fall">
    Prepare for colder weather by checking your heater and defroster.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Winter">
    Ensure your battery and ignition system are in good condition to withstand cold starts.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## DIY or Professional Help?

<Note>
Some tasks can be easily done at home, such as replacing wiper blades or air filters. For more complex maintenance, such as brake repairs or electrical issues, seek professional service.
</Note>

By adhering to a routine maintenance schedule, you not only ensure your vehicle's longevity but also its optimal performance. Always refer to your vehicle's owner's manual for specific recommendations and consult with a trusted mechanic for comprehensive inspections.

Remember, proactive maintenance is always better than reactive repairs.
