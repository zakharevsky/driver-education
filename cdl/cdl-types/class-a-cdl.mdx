## Decoding Class A CDL

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Defining a Class A CDL">
    A Class A CDL is your key to operating combination vehicles with a Gross Combination Weight Rating (GCWR) over 26,001 pounds, where the towed vehicle itself is over 10,000 pounds.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Comparing CDL Classes">
    Class A stands apart from Class B and C by allowing you to drive the heaviest commercial vehicles, perfect for long-haul trucking and transporting large loads.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Your Class A CDL Journey

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Basic Eligibility">
    Meet the age, medical, and legal standards set by your state's DMV.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Secure a CDL Learner's Permit">
    Clear a general knowledge test to get your CLP (CDL learner's permit), permitting supervised driving practice.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Undergo CDL Training">
    Join a CDL training program focused on Class A vehicles to develop necessary driving skills and knowledge.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ace Skills and Endorsement Tests">
    Pass the skills test, including pre-trip inspection, basic controls, and road test. Optional endorsements can broaden your driving capabilities.
  </Step>
</Steps>
