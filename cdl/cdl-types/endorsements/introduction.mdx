## CDL Endorsements

CDL endorsements enable you to operate special types of commercial motor vehicles (CMVs). Beyond the standard Class A, B, or C CDL requirements, specific endorsements require additional tests.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="H Endorsement (Hazardous Materials)">
    Required for vehicles carrying hazardous materials. Involves passing a [written knowledge test](/essentials/written-tests/hazmat). Learn more about the [HazMat endorsement](/cdl-types/endorsements/hazmat).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="N Endorsement (Tank Vehicle)">
    For operating tank vehicles transporting liquids or gases. Requires a [written knowledge test](/essentials/written-tests/tankers). Learn more about the [Tanker endorsement](/cdl-types/endorsements/tanker).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="P Endorsement (Passenger Vehicle)">
    Necessary for vehicles seating 16 or more, including the driver. Involves both [written](/essentials/written-tests/passenger) and road skills tests. Learn more about the [Passenger endorsement](/cdl-types/endorsements/passenger).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="S Endorsement (School Bus)">
    For school bus drivers, requiring a P endorsement as a prerequisite. Includes a [written test](/essentials/written-tests/school-bus), road test, application forms, fees, and a background check. Learn more about the [School Bus endorsement](/cdl-types/endorsements/school-bus).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="T Endorsement (Double and Triple Trailer)">
    Allows towing of double or triple trailers. Requires a [written knowledge test](/essentials/written-tests/doubles-triples). Learn more about the [Double/Triple Trailer endorsement](/cdl-types/endorsements/doubles-triples).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="X Endorsement (Hauling HazMat freight via tanker truck)">
    A combination endorsement for hazardous materials and tank vehicles. Involves a written knowledge test. 
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>


> **Tip**: Always check for additional endorsements specific to your state.

## CDL Restrictions

CDL restrictions can limit the operation of certain CMVs. The specific restriction code is noted on your CDL.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="E Restriction (Manual Transmission)">
    Prohibits operating vehicles with a manual transmission if tested with an automatic.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="K Restriction (Interstate Travel)">
    Prohibits travel to other states while driving a CMV.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="L Restriction (Air Brakes)">
    Disallows the operation of vehicles with a full air brake system unless tested on one.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="M Restriction (Class A Passenger Vehicle)">
    Limits drivers to operating Class B or C passenger vehicles or school buses, depending on the vehicle used for testing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="N Restriction (Class C Passenger Vehicle)">
    Restricts drivers to operating only Class C passenger vehicles or school buses, based on the testing vehicle class.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="O Restriction (Fifth-Wheel Connection)">
    Prohibits driving Class A vehicles with a non-fifth wheel connection.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="V Restriction (Medical Variance)">
    Indicates a medical variance as reported by the FMCSA, including conditions like diabetes or hearing impairment.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Z Restriction (Air Brakes)">
    Prohibits operation of CMVs equipped with full air brakes unless tested on one.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Understanding these endorsements and restrictions is crucial in defining the scope of your driving capabilities and ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements. Always stay informed about the specific requirements and limitations in your state to make the most of your CDL.
