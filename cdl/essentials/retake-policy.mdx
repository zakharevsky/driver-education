If you're preparing for your CDL exams, it's important to understand the retake policy. This ensures you're fully prepared for all possibilities on your journey to becoming a licensed commercial driver.

  <Card title="Initial Test Attempt" icon="clipboard-check" color="#4CAF50">

  <Card title="Retake Eligibility" icon="gear" color="#F44336">

  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Retake Intervals

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="First Attempt">
    After your initial test, a mandatory wait time may be imposed before a retake.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Second Attempt">
    If a second attempt is necessary, the wait time may increase to ensure adequate preparation.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Subsequent Attempts">
    Additional attempts often come with longer wait periods and possible remedial training requirements.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Fees and Limitations

- **Retake Fees**: Each retake might be subject to additional fees.
- **Testing Limits**: Some states limit the number of retakes within a certain time frame.

## Preparing for Success

<Steps>
  <Step title="Study and Review">
    After a failed attempt, review your study materials and practice tests to identify areas for improvement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remedial Training">
    Consider additional training or courses to bolster your knowledge and skills.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schedule Retake">
    Once you're ready and the waiting period has passed, schedule your retake with confidence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Remember, while the goal is to pass on your first try, understanding the retake policy helps manage expectations and plan your path to CDL success more effectively.
