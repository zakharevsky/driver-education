**What's the CDL Combination Test?**
- It's a specific part of the CDL exam focusing on operating combination vehicles (these typically consist of a combination of a tractor and one or more trailers). It's a multiple-choice test where you'll deal with questions about coupling and uncoupling trailers, air brakes, and the physics of a big rig. **Why It Matters**
- This isn't just about driving; it's about safety, mechanics, and logistics. Handling a combination vehicle requires skill and knowledge, which this test ensures you have.

## Understanding the CDL Combination Test

| Exam Feature           | Details                              |
|------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Number of Questions    | Typically 20-30 multiple-choice      |
| Passing Score          | 80% or higher                        |
| Time Limit             | Varies by state, often around 60 minutes |
| Key Topics             | Combination vehicle control, air brakes (if applicable), rollover risks, coupling and uncoupling, vehicle inspection |
| Test Format            | Computer-based in most locations     |
| Retake Policy          | Varies by state; often a waiting period and additional fee for retakes |
| Prerequisite           | Valid CDL; specific requirements may vary by state |

## Sample Combination Exam Questions

Test your knowledge with these sample questions.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Question 1: Rollover Risks">
    What factor most significantly increases the risk of rollover in a combination vehicle?
    - A) Type of cargo
    - B) Speed on turns and curves
    - C) Color of the vehicle
    - Correct Answer: B) Speed on turns and curves
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Question 2: Coupling">
    When coupling, the proper position of the fifth wheel is:
    - A) Tilted down towards the rear of the tractor
    - B) Tilted up towards the front of the tractor
    - C) Level with the ground
    - Correct Answer: C) Level with the ground
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## FAQs to Fuel Your Journey

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How Many Questions Are on the CDL Combination Test?">
    The number varies by state, but typically, you're looking at around 20-30 questions. It's a mix of multiple-choice and true/false.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What Does 'Combination' Mean in CDL?">
    In CDL terms, 'combination' refers to vehicles like tractor-trailers. It's about handling more than one unit, like a truck with a detachable trailer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What to Expect on the Actual Combination Exam?">
    Expect questions on vehicle inspection, coupling and uncoupling, and driving techniques. It's a test of both your knowledge and practical skills.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
